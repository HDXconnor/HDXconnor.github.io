AnguLAW

UALR Bowen Law School Hackathon 2016 - Winning Project

1st Place!

Eight teams in the Bowen hackathon were tasked with creating a legal application to assist the legal community – either lawyer or self-represented litigants.

The winning team, AnguLaw, consisted of Bowen law student Zachary Hale, Hendrix Computer Science students Connor Anderson and Collin Shaddox, IT professional Jeremy Robinson, and legal aid attorney Alexander Jones. AnguLaw created a web-based app that allows self-represented litigants to generate answers to filed complaints, thereby avoiding default judgments. They took home a $1,000 cash prize.

Difficulty Scaling in Chess AIs

Hendrix College Senior Thesis 2016

When playing chess against a computer, often times playing at one difficulty level is too easy while the next step up seems impossible to win against. This leads to an inability or stagnation of one's development. This thesis attempts to explore a system that better tailors its play to its opponents skill. I propose that the agent analyzes the performance of its opponents not based on the result of a game, but over the three major phases: Opening, Middlegame, and Endgame. Play is then adjusted for each section upon rematch.
